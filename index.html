Function of Barrage

The barrage is inserted on both sides of the neck connecting the melter and the working end. It reduces the temperature of the glass flowing and improves its homogeneity. The expected effects are:

  • Working-end temperature reduction
  • Bottom temperature increase in melting/refining zones
  • Energy consumption reduction
  • Blocking of surface current
  • Improved homogenization

Problem Statement

Special events and water leaks in the barrage led to ~600 tons of glass loss. The issues impacted production quality, yield, and defect rates. Primary goals:

  • Reduce special events
  • Eliminate water leaks
  • Standardize barrage operation and maintenance

Step 1: Initial Cleaning & Inspection

Activities:

  • Identifying 7 types of abnormalities
  • Visual inspections and technical displays
  • Tagging trends and analysis
  • Team assigned for AM implementation

Step 2: Countermeasures (Kaizen)

Examples of countermeasures:

  • Mini barrage installed to prevent corrosion
  • Flow sensor corrections and installations
  • Bypass valves and temperature gauges added
  • Standardized penetration depth using scales
  • Color coding and hose upgrades

Step 3: Standardization

Implemented standards:

  • Hydro and radiographic testing for barrage
  • Standardized immersion depths and spacing
  • Visual CILT checklists implemented
  • Savings and safety metrics recorded and tracked
